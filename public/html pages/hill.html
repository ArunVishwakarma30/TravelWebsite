Top Hill Stations to Visit Near Mumbai

Mumbai is blessed with some captivating hill stations near its city limits. An hour or two's drive is all it's going to take to reach some of the best hill stations near Mumbai, in Maharashtra, for a serene weekend getaway. People from the city often pack their bags to check out some of the best trekking spots and even visit hill stations for a break from all the heat and noise of the city. Needless to say, these hill stations near Mumbai do promise tranquillity and a mesmerising experience that would make you want to keep visiting them. We have listed out some of the top hill stations near Mumbai that you should definitely check out.

Hill Stations In Mumbai

1. Panchgani

Address

Panchgani, Maharashtra 412805 India (IN).

Panchgani
Smackdown in the middle of five hills lies the exquisite hamlet of Panchgani. The gorgeous hill station near Mumbai is considered to be one of the best in Maharashtra. People flock over from various parts of the state to enjoy the cold breeze and great scenery that the hill station has to offer. There are a plethora of things that you can do in Panchgani such as stargazing, camping, tourist sightseeing and more. It is one of the most popular and best hill station near Mumbai that you can visit.

2. Mahabaleshwar

Address

Mahabaleshwar Hill Station 412806 Maharashtra India

Mahabaleshwar
Another popular hill station near Mumbai is Mahabaleshwar. Often touted to be one of the best places for honeymoon in Maharashtra, Mahabaleshwar is a haven for tourists and couples. You can have a great time here and even turn your trip into a romantic sojourn as the hill station is blessed with many tourist attractions and sunrise and sunset points. You can rest assured that the place would leave you with a feeling of joy, thanks to its beauty and serenity!

3. Jawahar

Address

Jawahar, Maharashtra 401603, India

Jawahar
Often missed by tourists and travellers in Maharashtra, Jawahar is also one of Maharashtra's best contributions of hill stations near Mumbai. The enchanting hill station is surrounded by exquisite greenery and waterfalls. In fact, it is one of the few places in Maharashtra where you can find many gorgeous waterfalls. During your trip, you should add Jai Vilas Palace and the Jawahar Fort in your itinerary list. You can also trek up to the sunset point and the Hanuman point to immerse yourself in the tranquil vibe of the place. The landscape is certainly scintillating, making Jawahar one of the best hill stations near Mumbai.

4. Karjat

Address

Kaziranga National Park, Assam 785609 India

Karjat
This park has the largest population of one horned Rhino in the World. This national park has achieved great success with its conservation projects.
Things to do: Check out the tea plantations around the park Go on a safari to spot rhinos and tigers, Take in a view of the entire park from the watchtowers
Species to see: Tigers, Rhinos, Elephants, Swamp deer, Wild Buffalo

5. Bandhavgarh National Park

Address

Bandhavgarh National Park – Madhya Pradesh 484664 India

Bandhavgarh National Park
This Park has a large population of tigers and langurs. The park is at the center of India and is home to the famous white tiger.
Things to do: Go for a birding tour or a jeep safari to catch a glimpse of the best wildlife. Also once there, do not miss out on seeing the Khajuraho temples. Also, have a look at the Bandhavgarh fort.
Species to see: Bengal tigers, Elephants, deers, primates, reptiles and a large number of birds

6. Hemis National Park

Address

Hemis National Park – Ladakh 194106 Inida

Hemis National Park
This is a high altitude national park in eastern Ladakh. The park is home to many endangered animals like the snow leopard, the Asiatic Ibex, the red fox, Tibetan wolf. This park has pine forests, meadows and is nothing short of a safari in heaven.
Things to do: Take an adventure trek at Hemis
Species to see: snow leopard, the Asiatic Ibex, the red fox, Tibetan wolf, Himalayan marmot, Blue sheep

7. The Great Himalayan National Park

Address

The Great Himalayan National Park – Himachal Pradesh 175123 India

The Great Himalayan National Park
This park is in the snowcapped mountain regions of Kullu. With coniferous forests, sloping hills and green landscapes, this park is the crowning glory of the Himalayan slopes.
Things to do: Take a walk among the clouds, breathe in fresh pollution free air, go on a trek among the forest lands and take a boat ride on the river Tirthan. Also camp under the stars
Species to see: The snow leopard, musk deer, primates, Himalayan pit viper

8. Tadoba National Park

Address

Tadoba National Park – Maharashtra India.

Tadoba National Park – Maharashtra
Tadoba is one of the largest national parks in Maharashtra. Very close to the city, Nagpur, Tadoba is most famous for its Tigers and Tadoba crocodiles.
Things to do: Go on the tiger safari and bird watching trails
Species to see: The Chimur Hills and Andhari sanctuary
