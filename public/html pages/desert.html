MOST POUPULAR DESERTS TO VISIT IN INDIA

Whenever we think about going on a holiday, the first images that pop up in our minds are either hill stations or beach-side destinations. If someone were to tell you that perhaps a desert would be a nice change of pace, you wouldn’t take that seriously, would you? But here we are, trying to tell you otherwise. Apart from historically rich places and quaint hill stations, India is also home to many deserts. While they might look morose, the opportunities that await you are many. The vast landscapes are visually and aesthetically pleasing that make for the perfect picturesque vacation and amazing pictures. Right from the Thar desert to the cold plains of Ladakh, the country has a wide variety of deserts that suit everyone’s needs and holidaying plans, including yours. Still don’t believe us? Have a look at some of the most fascinating deserts in India and then see how your opinion changes!

Deserts in India

1. Jaisalmer

Address

Jaisalmer, Rajasthan, India (IN), Pin Code:-345001

Jaisalmer
Located amidst the Thar Desert, the largest desert in India is the Golden city of Jaisalmer! Let’s start the journey with this desert. You will find yourself in a land dotted with golden dunes and castles clad in red, things that are pretty normal in this destination. Fun fact? You can see your clear reflection in the clear, ornate lakes and get lost inside the mammoth archaic Havelis. If you feel like exploring the unending plane, you can surely do so on a camel’s back. And if you don’t like the image of bobbing up and down, you can always rent a jeep and drive through the sands.

2. Kutch

Address

Kutch Gujarat, India, Pin Code 370201

Kutch
When we read about deserts in our school books, didn’t we picture a desert as a never-ending land of golden sand? Here in India, you get a white one too! Located in the state of Gujarat, the Great Rann of Kutch comprises the largest salt plain in the country and is often called the white salt desert. When the temperature drops, the desert transforms into this vibrant and colourful place, waiting just for you to come and experience it all. What makes it even more interesting is the Rann festival. You can be a part of the famous Rann festival, with locals and other visitors, and immerse in the culture through various cultural activities. Not your cup of tea? No issues! You can always leave the crowd and bask in the solace one gets in the sky, on a hot-air balloon! What else? Instead of enjoying the sun on the shore, you can take a camel ride along the shoreline at Mandvi Beach. If you are done with all the fun and frolic of the hot-air balloon rides and camel rides and seek some knowledge, head to the Dholavira excavations to get a live history lesson

3. Spiti

Address

Spiti Himachal ,Pradesh ,pincode 17211 India

Spiti Valley
An addition to the cold desert category is Spiti, a famous desert in India. Where is this one situated? In Himachal Pradesh. Long winding roads welcome you into Spiti as you catch glimpses of the snow-capped mountain peaks. So, you know, a journey to Spiti is going to be beautiful! If you are an adventure freak and want to visit a desert, this is the best choice for you. The valley offers a variety of trekking trails that take you through the unexplored and untouched landscapes that are beautiful, to say the least. If you look for some peace, you can head to the many monasteries as the valley is known to be a religious centre for Buddhists. You can visit the Kye Gompa monastery to experience the sacred while you revel in the sheer size of the structure. If the sensational views from down below are not enough, get on the mountain ropeway from Kibber to Chichum as you glide mid-air and enjoy the panoramic views of the gorgeous gorge below you.

4. Ladakh

Address

Ladakh, Leh, 194101 India

Ladakh
This name was bound to appear on our list. After all, many consider it to be the best desert in India! Ladakh is replete with picturesque landscapes and the perfect weather for summer respite. When you are in the serene land, stop at Pangong Lake. It is a unique attraction as it is considered a colour-changing lake. It would be a rather suspenseful visit as you keep guessing which colour you will come across! After the lake, or before visiting the lake, a stop at the Khardung La Pass can be worthwhile, especially if you want to do some mountain biking around the snowy plains. What else? The many gompas, adorned by prayer flags fluttering in the chilly wind, will offer you the perfect spot to spend some quality hours in peace and serenity. Time for some fun! You can indulge in many adventure activities like climbing, jeep tours, rafting, and trekking.

5. Jodhpur

Address

Jodhpur, Rajasthan, 342006 India

Jodhpur
Another city that is nestled in the Great Thar Desert, Jodhpur. If you can scale to the majestic Mehrangarh Fort, you will be able to see the diminutive town draped in blue, hence its nickname ‘Blue City of India’. After capturing a snapshot of the blue landscape for your Instagram, you can head to Osian for the perfect desert safari experience. It is also adorned by the presence of some ancient ruins, which certainly adds to the charm. Despite all these wonderful spots to visit in Jodhpur, the actual magic remains in the archaic charm that exudes from the people and the little markets. The little shops around the clock tower and Sardar Market, bustling with locals and tourists alike, is what you will take away from this trip. So be sure to visit this beautiful part of the biggest desert in India, Thar! We told you, deserts are beautiful! What are you waiting for? Plan a trip to any of these deserts and get going. You won’t regret it.

6. The Thar Desert

Address

Thar desert, jaisalmer, Rajasthan, 345001 Inida

The Thar Desert
Thar desert is the main desert of western India and all other deserts there its subparts. It has an area of 200,000 km2 (77,000 sq mi) making it the 17th largest desert in the world and 9th largest subtropic desert. Its 75 percent area is in India and rest is in Pakistan. Basically, most of the area of Rajasthan is in Thar desert. Thar desert consists of all kinds of subtropical desert landscape. Its 10 percent area is sand dunes and the rest consists of craggy rocks, grasslands, and dry salt lake patches. The weather in Thar desert stay very hot mostly and reaches up to 50-degree Celcius and at night it can be lower than freezing point too

7. Pushkar

Address

Pushkar Ajmer, Rajasthan, 305022 India

Pushkar
Pushkar is located in Ajmer district in the state of Rajasthan. It is basically a part of Thar Desert and the most of terrain in and near the town is sand dunes and craggy rocks. It is a religious place which is visited by Sikhs and Hindus. It has Gurudwaras dedicated to Guru Nanak and Guru Gobind Singh and has the only Hindu temple dedicated to lord Bramaha, which is named as Jagatpita Brahma Mandir. Pushkar is also famous for hosting Pushkar fair every year, which attracts a lot of tourists throughout the year. In Pushkar tourist can also get camel rides too.

8. Bikaner

Address

Bikaner , Rajasthan, 334001 India.

Bikaner
Bikaner is located in the northwest region of the state of Rajasthan. It is a city with an area of 155 km2 (60 sq mi) and a population of 644,406 as per 2011 census. It is one of the oldest cities in Rajasthan and consists of a terrain having sand dunes, where tourist can enjoy camel rides. Bikaner is also known of Rats temple, which is very unique. In the month of May temperature in Bikaner can reach up to 50 degrees and at night it can get as cold as -4 degrees too. Bikaner has various temples and palaces too, which become a great attraction for tourist.

9. Nubra Valley

Address

Nubra Valley, Rajasthan 194101 India

Nubra Valley
Nubra Valley is located in the state of Jammu and Kashmir. It is 150 km away from Leh Town. It also consists of barren mountains and is a cold desert. It is 10,000 ft. or 3048 meters above sea level and this high altitude make this landscape a barren mountain land. Alongside huge mountains, Nubra valley also has some white sand dunes too. It is a very isolated area as compared to the Spiti Valley and otherwise, it is more or less the same. It also gives the view of the beautiful night sky in the mountains. Nubra valley is mostly closed during winters when there is heavy snowfall.

10. Khimsar

Address

Khimsar Rajasthan, 341025 India

Khimsar
Khimsar is located in Rajasthan and is a small village. It is 90 km away from Jodhpur and Bikaner highway and is popular for hosting Nagaur festival every year. Alongside the Nagaur festival, the main attraction of Khilmsar is the 450 years old Nagaur fort, which is awarded “National grand heritage award for excellence”. All of these alongside sand dunes attracts a lot of tourists to Khimsar making it a very popular destination among all of the people living in Rajasthan.
